I've spent nearly **30 years** building technology infrastructure that lets organizations grow, and these days that same drive for building has spilled into AI and game development too.

I spent six years leading IT infrastructure for a private equity-backed HVAC company in Tucson, Arizona, helping take the organization from **$20 million to $98 million** in revenue. I architected and built the entire infrastructure stack from the ground up: zero-trust security on a Fortinet stack, device management for **500+ users**, and the network foundation that let the company keep scaling. I put automation in place across onboarding, turning a 30-minute manual scripting task into a repeatable pipeline. I championed AI adoption company-wide, wrote the AI usage policy, piloted Microsoft Copilot, then deployed Claude for Teams integrated with Microsoft 365 and Entra ID, leading a Center of Excellence team that drove adoption across service operations, HR, marketing, and IT leadership.

That same curiosity is what's behind my current projects.

**Sector Lords** is a browser-based cyberpunk crime-war strategy game I built, inspired by the classic 1990s overlord strategy genre (Chaos Overlords vibes) — an original build made with Grok Build and Grok 4.5.

**Corsairs & Corps** is a 3D space simulation MMO-lite I'm building through agentic coding, using Codex, Unity MCP, and Obsidian to manage project state and documentation.

**Hoplon Defense** is an AI gateway webapp: a company AI chat layer with privacy, governance, multi-model routing, and token compression built in. It's part of a larger project helping companies map their operational workflows, surface operational risk, and figure out where AI can be applied intelligently and safely.

I also built **AI Models Made Useful**, a training guide to help people actually learn how to use AI well.

I love learning. Figuring things out and building real solutions is what gets me going, and right now that means going deep with Grok/Grok Build, Claude, ChatGPT/Codex, and Obsidian — whatever tool fits the problem.

If you're building something, let's talk.
